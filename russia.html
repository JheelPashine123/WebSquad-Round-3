January ‚ñº

New Year Holidays (Jan 1-8)

The biggest holiday celebration in Russia, including gift-giving, "New Year trees" (Novogodnyaya yolka), and visits from Ded Moroz (Grandfather Frost).

Orthodox Christmas (Jan 7)

A deeply religious holiday following the Julian calendar, celebrated with family feasts and church services.

Old New Year (Jan 13-14)

An informal, traditional holiday marking the New Year according to the old Julian calendar, serving as a relaxed, nostalgic end to the holiday season.

Epiphany (Jan 19)

A major Orthodox Christian holiday famous for the tradition of "ice-hole bathing," where people dip into freezing, cross-shaped pools of water.

February ‚ñº

Defender of the Fatherland Day (Feb 23)

A public holiday that originally honored the military but has evolved into an unofficial "Men's Day," celebrating all men.

Maslenitsa (Pancake Week) (Feb 24 - Mar 2)

A folk festival marking the end of winter. It involves eating blini (pancakes), folk celebrations, and burning an effigy of "Lady Maslenitsa" to welcome spring.

March ‚ñº

International Women's Day (Mar 8)

A major public holiday to celebrate all women with gifts, flowers, and special attention, similar to Mother's Day and Valentine's Day combined.

April ‚ñº

Orthodox Easter (Apr 20)

The most important religious holiday in the Orthodox faith. Celebrations include midnight church services, painting eggs, and baking special kulich (Easter bread).

May ‚ñº

Spring and Labour Day (May 1)

A public holiday marked with parades, concerts, and family outings to celebrate the arrival of spring.

Victory Day (May 9)

A very important national holiday commemorating the victory over Nazi Germany in WWII. It features massive military parades, especially in Moscow, and fireworks.

White Nights Festival (Late May - July)

A famous arts festival in St. Petersburg during the season of the midnight sun, including ballet, opera, and music events.

June ‚ñº

Russia Day (Jun 12)

The national holiday of the Russian Federation, celebrated with concerts, official ceremonies, and fireworks.

November ‚ñº

Unity Day (Nov 4)

A public holiday commemorating a 17th-century popular uprising that ended a period of political crisis.

December ‚ñº

Russian Winter Festival (Mid-Dec - Mid-Jan)

A broad cultural festival, especially in Moscow, including ice sculptures, Christmas markets, and winter activities at parks like Gorky Park.
