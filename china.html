January ‚ñº

New Year's Day (Jan 1)

The standard Gregorian calendar New Year's Day, celebrated as a public holiday.

Laba Festival (Jan 7)

A traditional festival where people often eat special Laba congee (porridge) to celebrate.

Spring Festival (Chinese New Year) (Jan 28 - Feb 4)

The most important traditional festival. It marks the start of the Lunar New Year with family reunions, feasts, fireworks, and red decorations for good luck.

February ‚ñº

Lantern Festival (Feb 12)

Marks the end of the Spring Festival celebrations. People light and watch lanterns, guess lantern riddles, and eat tangyuan (sweet rice balls).

March ‚ñº

International Women's Day (Mar 8)

An observance where women often receive special recognition or a half-day off from work.

April ‚ñº

Qingming Festival (Tomb-Sweeping Day) (Apr 4-6)

A day for honoring ancestors. Families visit graves to clean them, make offerings, and also go on spring outings to fly kites.

May ‚ñº

Labour Day Holiday (May 1-5)

A public holiday celebrating workers, which is extended into a "Golden Week" to encourage travel.

Dragon Boat Festival (May 31)

Honors the ancient poet Qu Yuan. Celebrations include eating zongzi (sticky rice dumplings) and holding dragon boat races.

August ‚ñº

Qixi Festival ("Chinese Valentine's Day") (Aug 29)

Known as the "Chinese Valentine's Day". It celebrates the mythical annual reunion of the cowherd and weaver girl in Chinese folklore.

September ‚ñº

Ghost Festival (Sep 6)

A traditional festival where it's believed the spirits of the deceased visit the living. People make offerings of food and burn "joss paper" to honor their ancestors.

October ‚ñº

National Day (Golden Week) (Oct 1-8)

Celebrates the founding of the People's Republic of China. It's a major week-long holiday for travel, festivities, and concerts.

Mid-Autumn Festival (Oct 6)

A harvest festival celebrating the full moon. Families gather to admire the moon, eat mooncakes, and light lanterns.

Double Ninth Festival (Oct 29)

A traditional day to respect elders. Activities include climbing mountains, drinking chrysanthemum wine, and flying kites.

December ‚ñº

Dongzhi (Winter Solstice) (Dec 21)

Marks the shortest day of the year. Families celebrate by gathering for a special meal, often eating tangyuan (in the south) or dumplings (in the north).
