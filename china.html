A Calendar of Celebration

January

New Year's Day (Jan 1)

[cite_start]

The standard Gregorian calendar New Year's Day, celebrated as a public holiday[cite: 53].

Laba Festival (Jan 7)

[cite_start]

A traditional festival where people often eat special Laba congee (porridge) to celebrate[cite: 54].

Spring Festival (Chinese New Year) (Jan 28 - Feb 4)

[cite_start]

The most important traditional festival[cite: 55]. [cite_start]It marks the start of the Lunar New Year with family reunions, feasts, fireworks, and red decorations for good luck[cite: 56].

February

Lantern Festival (Feb 12)

[cite_start]

Marks the end of the Spring Festival celebrations[cite: 58]. [cite_start]People light and watch lanterns, guess lantern riddles, and eat tangyuan (sweet rice balls)[cite: 59].

March

International Women's Day (Mar 8)

[cite_start]

An observance where women often receive special recognition or a half-day off from work[cite: 61].

April

Qingming Festival (Tomb-Sweeping Day) (Apr 4-6)

[cite_start]

A day for honoring ancestors[cite: 63]. [cite_start]Families visit graves to clean them, make offerings, and also go on spring outings to fly kites[cite: 64].

May

Labour Day Holiday (May 1-5)

[cite_start]

A public holiday celebrating workers, which is extended into a "Golden Week" to encourage travel[cite: 66].

Dragon Boat Festival (May 31)

[cite_start]

Honors the ancient poet Qu Yuan[cite: 67]. [cite_start]Celebrations include eating zongzi (sticky rice dumplings) and holding dragon boat races[cite: 68].

August

Qixi Festival ("Chinese Valentine's Day") (Aug 29)

[cite_start]

Known as the "Chinese Valentine's Day"[cite: 70]. [cite_start]It celebrates the mythical annual reunion of the cowherd and weaver girl in Chinese folklore[cite: 71].

September

Ghost Festival (Sep 6)

[cite_start]

A traditional festival where it's believed the spirits of the deceased visit the living[cite: 73]. [cite_start]People make offerings of food and burn "joss paper" to honor their ancestors[cite: 74].

October

National Day (Golden Week) (Oct 1-8)

[cite_start]

Celebrates the founding of the People's Republic of China[cite: 76]. [cite_start]It's a major week-long holiday for travel, festivities, and concerts[cite: 77].

Mid-Autumn Festival (Oct 6)

[cite_start]

A harvest festival celebrating the full moon[cite: 78]. [cite_start]Families gather to admire the moon, eat mooncakes, and light lanterns[cite: 79].

Double Ninth Festival (Oct 29)

[cite_start]

A traditional day to respect elders[cite: 80]. [cite_start]Activities include climbing mountains, drinking chrysanthemum wine, and flying kites[cite: 81].

December

Dongzhi (Winter Solstice) (Dec 21)

[cite_start]

Marks the shortest day of the year[cite: 83]. [cite_start]Families celebrate by gathering for a special meal, often eating tangyuan (in the south) or dumplings (in the north)[cite: 84].
